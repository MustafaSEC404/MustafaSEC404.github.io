CRITICAL

SQL Injection – Reservation Processing Endpoint

Business Risk: Potential full database disclosure, internal infrastructure enumeration, and exposure of sensitive reservation data.

Technical Insight: Unsanitized GET parameter directly incorporated into backend SQL query. Error-based responses confirmed injection on MySQL environment. Issue persisted after infrastructure-level mitigation, indicating missing application-layer fix.

Mitigation: Strict input validation, parameterized queries, suppression of verbose database errors.

Unauthenticated IDOR – Guest PII Exposure

Business Risk: Unauthorized disclosure of guest personal data (names, emails, phone numbers), enabling phishing campaigns and regulatory exposure.

HIGH

CSRF – Administrative Account Takeover

Business Risk: Silent password change for authenticated administrators, leading to full account compromise and persistence.

Technical Insight: Absence of CSRF tokens and lack of origin validation allowed external crafted request to modify account credentials.

Mitigation: Implement CSRF tokens, enforce SameSite cookie attributes, validate Origin/Referer headers.
